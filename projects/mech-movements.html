Background

For my Prototyping and Fabrication class in college, I was tasked with creating a physical mechanical model from one of the examples in 507 Mechanical Movements, a website containing drawings of various mechanical movements, such as gear, pulley and hydraulic assemblies.

I selected movement 127, a reciprocating rack and gear combo as my model base.

Description

After choosing movement 127, the reciprocating rack and pinion immediately reminded me of a seesaw, with the racks presenting two people moving up and down.

Before I could begin the fun part of stylizing the movement, I created a 2D drawing of the basic rack and pinion using Adobe Illustrator, to test the dimensions and orientations of the rack and gears necessary for the model to work. Using a laser cutter, I cut out all the necessary components for my first prototype using cardboard. Cardboard is a flimsy and pathetic material for gear building, but allowed me to verify the feasibility of my model. I used a spur gear builder website to image files for the rack and pinion for my desired pitch and toothcount.

I then moved on to creating a second prototype using wood. I faced a lot of difficult in getting the components to move smoothly together, and created a Solidworks model to find the best spacing between the racks and pinion.

After a lot of trial and error with dimensioning and tolerancing, I finally began to stylize the model. Instead of people, I imagined the racks as palm trees and the central gear as a sun. I plasma cut an aluminum see-saw doweled to the central gear, and added toucans on either side of the see-saw.
