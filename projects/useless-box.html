Background

The useless box is a machine designed to have no practical value. Upon pressing the switch, a finger will pop from under the lid to flick the switch back in protest, thus starting an endless cycle of turning on and turning back off again.

The useless box is also something I've been dying to build ever since I heard of the idea and browsed through many Instructables tutorials for inspiration. The box represents my first complete project with the Arduino, and provided me many adventures in soldering, laser cutting, and generally integrating electronic and mechanical components together.

Description

Overall, the box consists of a laser cut frame with two servo-controlled wooden "fingers" mounted inside. Pressing the power button near the base activates the whole system and turns on the red power LED. Upon pushing the metal switch, the first finger will lift the box lid, allowing the second finger to rise and push the switch back.

Instead of using a simple circuit, I controlled the box using an Arduino microcontroller, to customize the motions of the fingers. There are around five programmed movement sequences the fingers can take every time the switch is pressed, a few of them demonstrating how exasperated they are with the user disturbing their peace.

I initially prototyped the box using cardboard and a breadboard. The electronics were soldered to a perfboard once the circuit diagram was finalized. Determining the best placement of the servo fingers to press the switch and lift the lid proved difficult, since the box had limited space due to the battery pack and wires. After several iterations though, the useless box came alive!

After enduring a long fall semester during junior year, I picked a graveyard theme for the box and painted the fingers with a zombie-like green. I think the theme of death and irritation is relatable for all engineering students, but the project itself was a really fun outlet for me.
